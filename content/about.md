## 👨‍🔬 Who I Am

I'm a quantitative researcher and algorithm engineer with a deep passion for **applying advanced mathematics to financial markets**. My journey has been anything but conventional—it's been driven by curiosity, family commitment, and an unrelenting desire to build things that matter.

## 🎓 Academic Foundation

My research path began at **KU Leuven** (Belgium), where I earned both my Bachelor's and Master's degrees with **Honors**. During my time at **IMEC** (the world-leading nanoelectronics research center), I developed a strong foundation in signal processing and mathematical modeling—skills that now form the core of my market research.

> *"The same mathematics that describes physical systems can reveal profound truths about financial markets."*

My path—from semiconductor inspection to autonomous driving perception, and now to quantitative finance—has taught me that **core mathematical principles transcend domains**. Whether detecting a nanometer-scale defect or identifying a market regime shift, the underlying challenge is the same: separating signal from noise.

## 🔬 Research Philosophy

I believe that financial markets, despite their apparent randomness, exhibit **underlying geometric and spectral structures** that can be uncovered through rigorous mathematical analysis. My current work focuses on:

- 📊 **Spectral methods** (Fourier, Laplace, wavelet transforms) for market cycle identification
- 🧮 **Information geometry** and manifold learning for asset behavior classification
- ⚙️ **Building robust, automated trading systems** that execute strategies with discipline

## 🎯 What I'm Building Here

This blog documents my journey in applying physics-inspired mathematics to real market data. You'll find:

- Empirical validations of spectral clustering (like the first post)
- Explorations of multi-scale market dynamics
- Discussions on strategy design and risk management
- Occasional deep dives into the mathematics behind the models

All views expressed here are my own, and all code remains proprietary—but the methodology and insights are shared to foster discussion with like-minded researchers.
